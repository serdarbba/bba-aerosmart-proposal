Transparency
Scope Limitations
The following use cases have technical or physical limitations. We transparently share what we cannot do or where our scope is limited.
Sea-Level Monitoring — Not Feasible
Centimeter-precision sea level measurement via drone camera is physically impossible. This requires tide gauge sensors or LiDAR-based measurement.
Alternative we can offer: Approximate visual level change detection via fixed reference points (±20-30cm precision) and long-term indirect trend extraction from shoreline tracking data. Provides operational awareness, not scientific measurement.
Coastal Inspection — Limited Scope
BBA specializes in computer vision, not coastal engineering.
We can do: General visual anomaly detection (color change, shape deformation, new/missing objects), baseline comparison with change maps.

We cannot do: Engineering-grade crack analysis, corrosion classification, structural risk assessment. Scope can be extended based on domain-specific labeled data provided by Aerosmart.

Why Add Fixed Camera Support?

Drone patrols provide powerful wide-area scanning capability. Fixed cameras can serve as a complementary layer to fill coverage gaps between patrol flights — ensuring continuous monitoring at critical points while the drone covers wider areas. BBA's AI server can process both drone and fixed camera feeds through the same inference engine, maximizing coverage without additional algorithm development cost.
